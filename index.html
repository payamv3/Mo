Do you have an old Smartphone that nobody is using? Here are your options!

Resell: You could earn some cash by selling your old phone if in working condition, and can hold charge for a day’s use. Try the following websites to get an estimate of your smartphone’s current worth:

    • BackMarket - Online marketplace for refurbished electronics
    • Gazelle - Quick cash for your old devices

    It’s easy to resell, either vendor will send you a box with prepaid postage.

    Donate: You phone may not fetch a high resell value, but if it is still working and holding a charge, donating it will give it a new life. You can try donating your device, for example these organizations that have drop-off sites around the U.S.

    • Goodwill - Accepts working electronics at all locations
    • Salvation Army - Electronics donation accepted

    Recycle: If your phone does not work or if you do not want to resell or donate, you can bring it for recycling, for example:

    • Best Buy – Free electronics recycling at all stores, usually there is a bin near Customer Service

    IMPORTANT: WIPE YOUR DATA FIRST! Before giving up your smartphone, remember to completely erase your data from it. For Apple, this includes disabling Find My; for Android, this includes removing the Google Account from the device.

    • For Android Devices

    For Apple/iPhone devices:

    • Disable Find My
    • Erase iPhone

    Note: Sometimes it becomes too difficult to impossible to erase your data. The phone may be non-functional. In these situations, you will have to decide for yourself if you feel comfortable recycling or donating phones.
